# Issue 49 archive request pack

Last verified: 24 July 2026

This pack covers the issue #49 items that cannot be closed by public catalogue records, OCR or secondary publication alone. Each request asks for enough provenance to distinguish an original, a copy, a typescript, a later transcript and a published facsimile.

Do not mark an item complete merely because an institution confirms that it holds a related collection. Completion requires the requested object metadata and the necessary leaves or a clear written statement that access or reproduction is unavailable.

## Common evidence package

Ask every repository for the following, adapted to its terminology:

1. exact collection/fond, inventory/opis, unit or accession number;
2. official object title and date as catalogued;
3. material type: autograph, manuscript copy, typescript, carbon copy, photocopy or later transcript;
4. total number of leaves/pages and the institution's foliation;
5. colour images of every recto and verso, including covers, envelopes, inserts and blank sides bearing stamps or shelf marks;
6. object or accession card, dimensions, material, condition notes and provenance of acquisition;
7. restrictions on access, quotation, online publication and reproduction;
8. the institution's required citation form;
9. current reproduction price and expected delivery mechanism;
10. a written negative answer if the requested object is not held or cannot be identified.

For image files request lossless TIFF when available, otherwise the highest-resolution colour JPEG. Ask for a scale and colour target when the institution provides them.

### Subject

`Provenance inquiry: Tatyana Yakovleva–Mayakovsky letters and Roman Jakobson copies`

### Request text

Dear Houghton Library staff,

I am preparing a documentary and textual study of Vladimir Mayakovsky's correspondence with Tatyana Yakovleva. Published accounts state that seven letters were preserved through typescript copies supplied to Roman Jakobson and that Yakovleva herself made omissions before transmission.

Could you please determine whether Houghton Library or another Harvard repository holds any of the following:

1. a Tatyana Yakovleva du Plessix Liberman personal or family collection;
2. the relevant Roman Jakobson papers, correspondence files or deposited copies;
3. Mayakovsky letter typescripts, carbon copies, photocopies, translations or editorial transcripts;
4. Yakovleva's covering letters, notes about omissions or excised passages;
5. envelopes, postal evidence or acquisition correspondence;
6. a finding aid or accession record that identifies provenance and physical extent.

For every located item, please provide the collection and call number, folder title, date, physical format, leaf count, provenance, access restrictions, available recto/verso reproductions and the required citation.

A negative result would also be valuable: please state whether the public finding aids contain no separately catalogued Yakovleva collection or no Mayakovsky correspondence matching this description, and suggest any Harvard repository or external institution that should be contacted next.

Sincerely,
[Name]
[Affiliation or independent researcher]
[Contact details]

### Completion test

The item remains open until the autographs or transmission copies, omissions and envelopes are identified by call number and physical witness. A generic biographical collection description is insufficient.
